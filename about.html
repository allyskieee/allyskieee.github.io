About Me

Hi! I’m Allyssa Mae Alumno Locloc, but you can simply call me Ally. I’m a proud Criminology student from the University of the Cordilleras, following a path that balances both heart and purpose. While my field may deal with laws, justice, and the protection of others, my soul is quietly nurtured by life’s softer joys — like the aroma of baked goods, the comfort of rest, and the magic of shared meals.

In a world that often moves too fast, I find peace in slowing down. I believe beauty is found in life’s little things — the smell of banana cake in the oven, a quiet morning with coffee, the rhythm of rain on the window, and the comfort of being surrounded by people who matter.

My Hobbies

There’s something special about baking banana cake. It’s not just a recipe — it’s a memory. It reminds me of home, of rainy days spent inside, wrapped in a blanket, listening to soft music while the sweet smell of bananas fills the air. It’s more than a dessert — it’s a hug in the form of food.

Cookies are my playground for creativity. No two batches are ever the same — sometimes they’re soft and chewy, sometimes they’re crunchy with a little extra magic in the middle. I love experimenting with flavors and textures, but one thing stays the same: they’re always made with love (and maybe a few too many chocolate chips).

Food is more than fuel — it’s a language of love. It’s how I care for others, how I express gratitude, how I celebrate, and even how I cope. Cooking for someone or sharing a meal is my way of saying, “You matter.” I believe food connects us in ways words sometimes can’t.

Sushi, to me, is calm on a plate. Every roll feels thoughtful — neat, peaceful, balanced. Eating sushi makes me feel grounded, like everything in life can have a sense of order and beauty. It’s my way of treating myself, a small celebration that feels both elegant and peaceful.

My Inspiration: Family

At the heart of everything I do is my family. They are my strength, my source of peace, and my greatest reason for pushing forward. Their love has shaped who I am — strong, grounded, and full of heart. Whether I’m succeeding or struggling, I carry their lessons and support with me wherever I go.

They’re my reason, my peace, and my forever home. Every success is theirs, too. When I dream, I dream with them in mind. When I succeed, it’s a shared victory. They’re my constant, my calm, and the home I always carry inside me. No matter where life leads, my family will always be my anchor and my wings.
